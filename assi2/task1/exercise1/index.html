Diogenes of Sinope

The Original Cynic Philosopher

Diogenes of Sinope (c. 412 BCE – 323 BCE) was a Greek philosopher and one of the founders of Cynicism. Known for his sharp wit and radical rejection of social conventions, he lived in a barrel and valued virtue over wealth or fame.

Key Life Events

  1. c. 412 BCE – Born in Sinope (modern-day Turkey).
  2. Travelled to Athens and became a disciple of Antisthenes.
  3. Famously lived in a large ceramic jar (pithos) to demonstrate minimalism.
  4. Met Alexander the Great; told him to “stand out of my sunlight.”
  5. 323 BCE – Died at Corinth, leaving a legacy of fearless independence.

Philosophy & Contributions

  • Founder and exemplar of the Cynic movement.
  • Advocated living in accordance with nature.
  • Critiqued social pretension and materialism.
  • Influenced later Stoic philosophers.
